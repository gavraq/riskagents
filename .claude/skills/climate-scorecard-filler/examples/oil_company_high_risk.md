## SUMMARY & RECOMMENDATIONS

PetroNorth Energy demonstrates **VERY HIGH** climate and environmental risk (Overall Score: 3.8/5). The company operates in the **PROHIBITED** oil sands sector under the Bank's Environmental Risk Policy, which states "**no appetite**" for this activity.

**Transition risks are CRITICAL (4.2/5)**. Oil sands face existential threats from:
- Severe carbon pricing creating cost disadvantage vs conventional oil
- Technology disruption (EVs) threatening demand
- Investor divestment and market sentiment turning against oil sands
- Stranded asset risk with 30-year reserve life but demand potentially peaking within 5-10 years

**Physical risks are MEDIUM-HIGH (3.2/5)**, primarily driven by ecosystem degradation, wildfire exposure, and water stress.

**MANDATORY DOWNWARD RATING OVERRIDE REQUIRED: 2 NOTCHES**

**Recommendation:**
1. **DECLINE NEW EXPOSURE.** Oil sands are explicitly prohibited under Environmental Risk Policy Section 5.4. Any new transactions would require escalation to Board-level exception approval.

2. **EXISTING EXPOSURE - REDUCTION STRATEGY REQUIRED.** If the Bank has existing exposure:
   - Implement run-off strategy with no tenor extensions
   - No increase in notional or new product types
   - Require quarterly monitoring of transition plan credibility
   - Stress test exposure under IEA Net Zero 2050 scenario
   - Explore collateral alternatives or credit enhancement

3. **TRANSITION PLAN SCRUTINY.** If engagement continues:
   - Require credible decarbonization pathway (not currently visible)
   - Assess viability of carbon capture claims (likely uneconomic at scale)
   - Monitor capex allocation (if >50% to oil sands expansion, red flag)
   - Evaluate diversification into renewables or low-carbon energy

4. **REPUTATIONAL RISK MANAGEMENT.**
   - Any continued exposure creates NGO campaign risk
   - ESG ratings agencies will penalize bank for oil sands exposure
   - Investor relations impact for bank's own ESG positioning

**Credit Rating Impact:** Apply 2-notch downward override. If PetroNorth is currently rated IG11 (BBB), override to IG13 (BBB-). This reflects material increase in default risk from transition factors over medium term (5-10 years).

**Next Steps:**
- Escalate to Chief Risk Officer given prohibited sector
- Legal to review existing agreements for ESG-related termination provisions
- Business to develop run-off strategy if exposure exists
- Compliance to assess reputational risk and stakeholder impact

## MITIGATION MEASURES IN PLACE

**ASSESSMENT: INADEQUATE**

Claimed mitigation measures:
- ⚠️ **Carbon capture targets** - Company claims 30% emissions reduction by 2030 via CCS, but CCS uneconomic at current carbon prices; no operating projects at scale; likely aspirational
- ⚠️ **Operational efficiency** - Incremental improvements insufficient to offset policy headwinds
- ⚠️ **Methane reduction** - Regulatory compliance minimum, not competitive advantage
- ⚠️ **Reclamation commitments** - Track record poor (0.2% certified reclamation); underfunded liability

**What's missing:**
- ❌ No credible pathway to align with Paris Agreement or Net Zero 2050
- ❌ No material capex reallocation to low-carbon business
- ❌ No diversification strategy into renewables
- ❌ Insufficient reclamation funding
- ❌ No scenario analysis showing business viability under IEA NZE or NGFS scenarios

**Conclusion:** Mitigation measures do not materially reduce climate risk exposure. Company business model incompatible with Paris Agreement goals.
