## SUMMARY & RECOMMENDATIONS

GCB Bank demonstrates **MODERATE** climate and environmental risk (Overall Score: 1.9/5). The bank's exposure is primarily **indirect** through its loan portfolio concentration in climate-sensitive sectors (agriculture 43%, commerce 18%) and geographic concentration in coastal Ghana.

**Transition risks are LOW (1.6/5)** given Ghana's current limited carbon pricing regime, gradual energy transition timeline, and the bank's domestic retail/SME focus. As a Financial Institution with minimal direct emissions, GCB's transition risk manifests through portfolio-level exposure to energy and carbon-intensive borrowers, which is currently limited.

**Physical risks are MODERATE (2.5/5)** with coastal flooding and agricultural climate impacts being the key concerns. Ghana faces increasing acute weather events (flooding, droughts) that affect borrower creditworthiness and collateral values. The agricultural sector's vulnerability to changing rainfall patterns and rising temperatures creates credit risk through potential NPL increases.

**NO DOWNWARD RATING OVERRIDE IS REQUIRED.** The overall climate risk score of 1.9/5 is consistent with the current RG24 rating. GCB's climate risk profile is comparable to peer Sub-Saharan African banks with similar geographic and sectoral concentrations.

**However**, ongoing monitoring of the following is recommended:
- **Agricultural loan performance** - Track NPLs in agricultural sector for climate-driven deterioration
- **Real estate collateral values** - Monitor coastal property valuations for climate event impacts
- **Sovereign climate risk** - 33% of assets are Ghanaian government securities; sovereign climate stress affects GCB
- **Regulatory developments** - Bank of Ghana may introduce climate risk management requirements

**Next Review:** February 2026 (annual) or upon material climate events (major flooding, severe drought affecting agricultural output)

## KEY RISK DRIVERS

- **Coastal flooding exposure** - Accra and coastal cities face increasing flood risk; real estate collateral and borrower operations vulnerable
- **Agricultural sector concentration** - 43% retail loans include significant agricultural exposure (cocoa, crops, livestock) vulnerable to changing rainfall patterns and temperature increases
- **Sovereign climate risk** - 33% of total assets in Ghanaian government securities; sovereign fiscal stress from climate adaptation costs or disasters would affect GCB's creditworthiness
- **Limited climate risk management capacity** - No dedicated climate risk function; limited climate scenario analysis or TCFD-style disclosure
- **Indirect portfolio exposure** - Borrower climate vulnerabilities transmit to GCB through defaults and collateral depreciation

---

## MITIGATION MEASURES IN PLACE

- **Diversified loan portfolio** - Lending across retail (43%), services (26%), commerce (18%) reduces single-sector climate risk concentration
- **Strong regulatory capital buffers** - 16% Total CAR (3Q24) provides cushion for potential climate-driven credit losses; Bank of Ghana forbearance extends to 2026
- **Adequate loan loss provisions** - Estimated 80.7% NPL coverage ratio (3Q24) provides buffer for deteriorating borrower credit quality
- **Government ownership (51%)** - State ownership provides implicit support for systemic shocks including climate-related stress; D-SIB status enhances likelihood of government support
- **High cash holdings (21% of assets)** - Strong liquidity position allows flexibility to support borrowers during climate events
- **Bank of Ghana oversight** - Central bank conducts stress testing which may increasingly incorporate climate scenarios

---

## MONITORING & REVIEW TRIGGERS

- **Annual review** required given moderate physical risk score and sector exposures
- **Material climate events** - Update scorecard within 30 days following major floods, droughts, or coastal storms affecting Ghana
- **Agricultural NPL deterioration** - Review if agricultural sector NPLs increase >25% YoY or if cocoa sector shows material stress
- **Sovereign climate stress** - Update if Ghana sovereign rating downgraded for climate-related fiscal stress or climate-related government bond restructuring
- **Real estate collateral stress** - Review if coastal real estate valuations decline >20% due to flood events or chronic climate impacts
- **Regulatory developments** - Update upon Bank of Ghana introduction of climate risk management requirements or stress testing mandates
- **Portfolio concentration shifts** - Review if exposure to prohibited/restricted sectors exceeds 5% of total loans
