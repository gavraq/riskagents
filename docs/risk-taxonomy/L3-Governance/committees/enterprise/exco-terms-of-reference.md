## 1. Purpose

### 1.1 Role

The Meridian Global Bank Executive Committee ("the ExCo") is the senior executive governance committee of the Bank and has the responsibility for discharging the strategic objectives given to it by the Board within the appropriate risk, control, and regulatory framework.

The ExCo is ultimately responsible for ensuring that in pursuing these objectives the Bank's operations are carried out prudently, efficiently, and with due regard to its policies, standards, reputation, shareholders, as well as regulatory requirements.

### 1.2 Monthly Review

The ExCo will review, discuss, and/or approve matters and act within authority given and set by the Board. The ExCo will formulate, discuss, and escalate matters to the Board and shareholders if required or deemed appropriate.

Monthly, to discharge its responsibilities, the ExCo will examine the Bank's income, performance, capital, and risk profile.

### 1.3 Regulatory Context

Meridian Global Bank is regulated in the United Kingdom by the Financial Conduct Authority ("FCA") and Prudential Regulation Authority ("PRA").

ExCo has responsibility for overseeing the Bank and its branches and subsidiaries in relation to capital and other prudential and governance responsibilities.

## 2. Constitution

### 2.1 Board Sub-Committee

The ExCo is constituted as a sub-committee of the Board and has a direct reporting line.

### 2.2 Board Delegation

The Board has delegated to the Executive Committee certain powers to manage and administer the day-to-day activities of the Bank under the overall direction and supervision of the Board. Subject to such direction and supervision, the Board Reserved Matters, Shareholder Reserved Matters, the Shareholder Agreement, the Articles, the Executive Committee Mandate, and all applicable Law, the Board has resolved that the Management Team shall have the authority to manage the business of the Bank which shall be conducted in a manner consistent with the Business Plan and Budget.

The Board has approved the Chief Executive Officer to delegate accountability and authority for delivery against the Business Plan as they deem appropriate and in accordance with the Senior Managers and Certification Regime and designated Senior Manager Function holders.

### 2.3 Authority

The Committee's authority is derived from the Board which approves its mandate. This mandate and any future amendments will be approved by the Board.

## 3. Responsibilities and Delegations

### 3.1 Strategy and Planning

3.1.1 Formulation of strategy and plans (both financial and non-financial) to be reviewed and approved by the Board

3.1.2 Reviewing progress against strategy as approved by the Board: reviewing performance against agreed financial, non-financial, and operational targets set within the agreed risk appetite

3.1.3 Agree and recommend detailed business plans and budgets prior to their submission to the Board

3.1.4 Review the Bank's financial/management accounts

3.1.5 Reviewing cost management and subject to the Board's direction, manage costs as appropriate

### 3.2 Corporate Matters

3.2.1 Propose, initiate, review, and execute strategic initiatives including but not limited to acquisitions, alliances, mergers, disposals, and senior appointments requiring Board and/or Shareholders' approval per the Bank's Articles of Association and Shareholders' Agreement

3.2.2 Agree and recommend proposals for any new or closure of entities, for further approval by the Board and/or shareholders

### 3.3 Risk and Capital

3.3.1 Agreeing and recommending risk appetite for approval by the Board and delegating authority to sub-committees

3.3.2 Oversight of Capital management and liquidity planning, including review of the activities of the Asset and Liability Committee ("ALCO")

### 3.4 Culture and Conduct

3.4.1 Monitoring culture and conduct, taking appropriate actions to improve culture where necessary

3.4.2 Decisions undertaken or ratified by the ExCo should consider the impact any action, or inaction, may have on people and culture. This includes decisions made with regards to the forward-looking business approach as well as those taken retrospectively considering incidents or events, where behaviour should form part of the root cause analysis. ExCo will consider any concerns escalated by the Chairs of any sub-committee, with regards to the impact of any action or inaction on people or culture.

### 3.5 Regulatory and Compliance

3.5.1 Reviewing adherence to the Client Money and Asset ("CASS") regulations

3.5.2 Strategic management of Human Resources and reviewing related metrics and issues, including headcount, attrition, absenteeism, and key performance indicators

3.5.3 Review monthly reports on Outsourced Services and Supplier Risk Governance from the COO (as the Accountable Executive for Outsourcing, SMF 24), including relevant updates from the Global Supplier Risk Management Forum

3.5.4 Executive oversight of the Operational Resilience Programme and approval of the Operational Resilience Self-Assessment prior to its submission to the Board

3.5.5 Make material investment decisions, escalated via the Investment and Change Committee ("ICC") or otherwise, on suggested proactive and reactive remediation or change activities that impact operational resilience

3.5.6 Noting all matters escalated relating to reputational risk and initiating remedial action where necessary

### 3.6 ESG and Sustainability

3.6.1 Formulate, for agreement by the Board, the Bank's responsibilities and response to Environmental (including climate change), Social, and Governance ("ESG") matters, including:
- Developing the sustainability strategy and associated risk appetite for discussion and agreement by the Board, and updating the strategy where necessary based upon Board feedback
- Ensure progress is made on delivering the strategy within risk appetite and that the Board and Board Committees are kept up to date on progress
- Regularly monitor any sustainability targets and metrics (as set by the Board)

### 3.7 Stress and Contingency

3.7.1 Coordinating and managing the Bank's response to extreme stress in accordance with the Contingency ExCo Terms of Reference

### 3.8 Compliance Programme

3.8.1 Ensure the Bank has an effective compliance programme covering regulatory and financial crime risk including anti-money laundering, anti-bribery and corruption, counter terrorist financing, fraud, sanctions, and any other financial crimes

3.8.2 Reviewing material Compliance issues referred from the Chief Compliance Officer or Regulatory Compliance Committee

3.8.3 Reviewing such other matters as the Committee deems necessary to fulfil its responsibilities and as may be requested by the Board

### 3.9 Sub-Committee Oversight

3.9.1 Oversight of ExCo's subcommittees

3.9.2 Review reports and consider matters escalated from subcommittees

3.9.3 Reviewing and approving mandates and membership of executive sub-committees

3.9.4 Approving or delegating to sub-committees approval of policies

### 3.10 Policy Approval

3.10.1 To review and approve certain policies as detailed in Appendix B

3.10.2 To receive regular reports as listed in Appendix B

### 3.11 Annual Planning

3.11.1 Annually agree the reports to be received by the Committee and the plan of business for Committee meetings for the year ahead

### 3.12 Challenge and Accountability

3.12.1 Members should hold each other to account against the matters discussed and be able to challenge each other effectively and promptly

## 6. Committee Operation

### 6.1 Meeting Frequency

Meetings are held on a **monthly** basis, or as and when the Chair considers appropriate or necessary.

A **Contingency ExCo** can be called at the discretion of the Chair, or for instance when certain metrics or indicators have been breached and requires actions to be directed by the committee.

### 6.2 Independent Judgement

Each member should bring independent judgement to bear in considering matters presented to the Committee.

### 6.3 Information

All members are to receive all appropriate information on a timely basis.

### 6.4 Decision Making

The Chair will, where possible, evidence the approval of a decision by concluding that there is a consensus in favour of the decision and that the decision is therefore approved. In the event of no clear consensus, as determined by the Chair, a matter is required to be decided by a vote of the Committee. Any such matter shall be decided by a majority of votes. In the event of an equality of votes, the Chair shall have a casting vote.

### 6.5 Quorum

The quorum necessary for a valid meeting is at least **4 members**, including:
- The Chair (or Deputy Chair)
- The Chief Risk Officer (or delegate)
- At least 2 other members

### 6.6 Papers

Materials will be sent in advance of the Committee and will normally include:
- Agenda
- Meeting papers
- Minutes (where applicable)

This will be co-ordinated by the Chair.

## 8. Escalation

### 8.1 To Board

Should the Committee identify any risk concerns which fall outside their specific mandate, the Committee has the responsibility to escalate that concern to the Chair of the appropriate committee for full consideration. In the absence of a clear path of referral, the Committee should escalate to the Chair of the Board.

### 8.2 From Sub-Committees

The Committee shall on a timely basis consider and decide on all matters raised for its attention by any of its sub-committees and/or forums.

### 8.3 Business Heads

Business Heads shall be required, periodically, to update on their risk profile or strategy as directed by the Chair.

## 10. Contingency ExCo

### 10.1 Activation

A Contingency ExCo may be convened when:
- Significant market stress event occurs
- Material operational incident
- Regulatory emergency
- Liquidity stress event
- Reputational crisis

### 10.2 Authority

During a Contingency ExCo, the Committee has enhanced authority to:
- Implement contingency plans
- Approve emergency funding
- Authorise crisis communications
- Suspend normal delegations if required

### 10.3 Reporting

All Contingency ExCo decisions must be reported to the Board within 24 hours.
