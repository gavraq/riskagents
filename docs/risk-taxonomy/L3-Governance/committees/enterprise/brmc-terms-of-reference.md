## 1. Purpose

### 1.1 Role

The Board Risk Management Committee ("the Committee") is requested and authorised by the Board of the Bank ("the Board") to monitor and review any activity falling within its Terms of Reference as set out below and to investigate any matters as may otherwise be requested by the Board from time to time.

### 1.2 Board Delegation

The Board is primarily responsible for the governance of risk and compliance with legal and regulatory obligations. It delegates to management, in particular through the Chief Risk Officer for risk management and the Head of Compliance for compliance, the responsibility for the management of risk and compliance respectively, including the design, implementation, and monitoring of plans and processes.

The Board has delegated authority to the Committee to provide independent and objective oversight of risk management and compliance across the Bank.

### 1.3 Committee Objectives

In order to achieve this, the Committee must:

- Ensure that risk management and compliance policies and standards are well documented, up to date, and support the Bank's strategic objectives by being fit for purpose
- Review and assess the awareness and effectiveness of the risk management and compliance control frameworks
- Review and assess the adequacy and appropriateness of the risk appetite including the state of compliance with current and known future legal and regulatory obligations, to support the delivery of current strategic objectives
- Support a climate of discipline and control that will reduce the opportunity for fraud
- Provide challenge to management in their review and oversight of risk and compliance
- Hold management to account against the matters delegated and be able to challenge the executive effectively and promptly
- Monitor effective communication between the Head of Compliance, the management, and the regulators

---

## 2. Composition of the Committee

### 2.1 Membership

The Committee shall be comprised of not less than **3 Non-Executive Directors** of the Bank including at least **2 Independent Non-Executive Directors**. Each person shall be a Member.

| Role | Requirement | Voting |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Chair** | Independent Non-Executive Director | Yes |
| **Members** | At least 2 additional Non-Executive Directors | Yes |
| **Total Membership** | Minimum 3 | - |

### 2.2 Chair Appointment

The Chair of the Committee will be an Independent Non-Executive Director nominated by the Board and appointed by the Board (following review and recommendation by the Nomination Committee). The Chair will normally serve for a minimum term of 3 years.

In the absence of the Chair at any meeting, an alternative Independent Non-Executive Chair will be elected by a majority of Members present. The Chair shall not be the Chair of the Board or other Board Committees (unless acting on an ad-hoc basis to cover absence).

### 2.3 Replacement

The Board may at any time replace any Member of the Committee or its Chair, subject to the provisions of the Shareholders' Agreement.

### 2.4 Term

Members will be appointed by the Board and normally will be expected to serve for a minimum of 3 years.

### 2.5 Quorum

A quorum will be **3 Members** of whom:
- 2 will be Independent Non-Executive Directors
- 1 will be an Executive Director or Shareholder-appointed Director

### 2.6 Attendees

Attendees at meetings of the Committee will be by invitation of the Chair and may include any of, but shall not be limited to:

- Chief Executive
- Chief Risk Officer
- Chief Financial Officer
- Head of Compliance
- Company Secretary (as Secretary to the Committee)
- Other persons as requested by the Chair

---

## 3. Reporting Responsibilities

### 3.1 Reports to Board

The Committee reports to the Board. The Chair, any Member, or a person to whom such authority is delegated can report to the Board or relevant Board Committee or the Chair of those boards or committees.

### 3.2 Minutes

The minutes of the meetings will be taken by the Company Secretary. In the absence of the Company Secretary, his or her delegate shall take the minutes subject to the agreement of the Chair. The minutes shall be submitted to the Board.

### 3.3 Recommendations to Board

The Committee will provide advice and submit recommendations to the Board for its approval on the effectiveness of the risk and compliance frameworks, policies, and standards to support the delivery of the Bank's strategic objectives.

### 3.4 Regular Reporting

To support this, the Committee will also regularly provide at least:

3.4.1 An objective and critical assessment of the Bank's exposure to relevant risk types, as well as its state of compliance with all current and known future legal and regulatory obligations

3.4.2 The review and recommendation to the Board for approval of the various obligatory regulatory reports including:
- Operational Resilience Self-Assessment
- Individual Capital Adequacy Assessment Report (ICAAP)
- Individual Liquidity Adequacy Assessment Report (ILAAP)
- Recovery and Resolution Plan

3.4.3 The approval of the regulatory and internal stress tests to be applied against the projected future performance of the Bank

3.4.4 The review of the Bank's reverse stress testing programme and recommendation to the Board for approval

## 4. Authority

The Committee has the right to:

4.1 Access to any information that the Committee needs to fulfil its responsibilities providing this does not breach applicable law in any relevant jurisdiction

4.2 Seek independent advice from third parties

4.3 Investigate matters within its Terms of Reference

4.4 Call on the Board Chair, the Chief Executive, Executive Directors, Internal Auditor, Chief Finance Officer, Chief Risk Officer, Head of Compliance, and External Auditors and other competent persons as the Chair of the Committee thinks appropriate to provide whatever information in whatever format it requires

4.5 Call any member of staff (employee, contractor, or otherwise) to provide information to the Committee in person at a meeting as and when required

4.6 Form and delegate authority to sub-committees and may also delegate authority to one or more designated members of the Committee

## 5. Terms of Reference

The Committee is the highest authority on risk management and compliance below the Board in the Bank. The Committee, through its Chair, will report to the Board on all matters of significance pertaining to risk management and compliance.

The Board has delegated broad authority to the Committee to:

### 5.1 Risk Framework Oversight

a) Review, assess, and challenge the appropriateness and effectiveness of the Bank's approved risk and compliance frameworks, its risk appetite statement (including its appetite for regulatory and conduct compliance), the underlying policies, standards, and where appropriate overall risk type exposure limits

b) Approve and recommend for approval to the Board, changes to those frameworks, risk appetite statements, the underlying policies, and standards where the Committee believes those changes are appropriate in order to support better the delivery of the Bank's strategic objectives

c) Review the standards, integrity, and professionalism being adopted by the Bank in its risk management and compliance process and the adequacy of resources allocated to risk management and compliance and provide advice, oversight, and challenge necessary to embed and maintain a supportive risk culture throughout the Bank

### 5.2 Risk Monitoring

d) Oversee the current risk exposures, including capital and liquidity management

e) Oversee the formulation of a robust and prudent risk appetite statement (to include the appetite for regulatory and conduct compliance), its ongoing adherence, and to monitor the implementation of corrective actions in the event of breach

f) Ensure that strategic and tactical decisions taken by the businesses are fully reflected and updated in the Bank's risk appetite statement

g) Ensure continual risk and compliance monitoring and assessment by management which will include:
   - Monitor changes in internal and external environment
   - Determine impact of the current and prospective macroeconomic and financial environment drawing on financial stability assessments and changes on strategic risk profile
   - Analyse and learn lessons from trends and failures
   - Identify emerging risks

h) Ensure that it is properly informed on matters of risk and compliance through risk management and compliance reports, reports from both internal and external auditors, and correspondence with regulators, in order to support the assurance to the Board regarding the effectiveness of the risk management and compliance processes

i) Maintain a continuing liaison with the Chief Risk Officer and Head of Compliance who will report to the Committee on risk exposures and compliance with legal and regulatory obligations, relative to risk appetite

j) Define, challenge, and approve as appropriate any extent to which the risks inherent in any proposed business strategy and plans are consistent with the Bank's risk appetite including its appetite for legal and regulatory compliance

### 5.3 Independence and Effectiveness

k) Where requested by management, consider approval of or removal of either or both the Chief Risk Officer and Head of Compliance in accordance with regulations

l) Safeguard the independence and ensure the effectiveness of the compliance function

m) Safeguard the independence and ensure the effectiveness of the risk function

### 5.4 Policy Review

n) Review certain policy/other documents as listed in Appendix 1

o) Annually agree the reports to be received by the Committee and the plan of business for Committee meetings for the year ahead

## 6. Specific Risk Type Responsibilities

### 6.1 Credit Risk

The Committee will perform the following duties with respect to Credit Risk:

6.1.1 Review the credit portfolios, their concentrations, and trends including review and challenge of the results of scenario stress testing and associated management actions

6.1.2 Review the approvals of credits made by Credit Committee without Credit Department support

6.1.3 Review reports and indicators of stressed and distressed credits and management's actions to mitigate, manage, and avoid repetition of these risks

6.1.4 Review the adequacy of models, other analytics, methodologies, and systems to measure, manage, report, control, and aggregate credit risks

6.1.5 Review credit policy documents at the next meeting following approval by management

6.1.6 From time to time, undertake "deep drill" reviews on specific issues that the Board and/or this Committee deems appropriate in the light of prevailing market conditions

### 6.2 Market Risk

The Committee will perform the following duties with respect to Market Risk:

6.2.1 Review the market risk profile, VaR utilisation, and trends including review and challenge of the results of stress testing

6.2.2 Review backtesting performance and exception trends

6.2.3 Monitor adherence to market risk limits and review material breaches

6.2.4 Review the adequacy of VaR models, stress testing frameworks, and related methodologies

6.2.5 Review market risk policy documents at the next meeting following approval by management

### 6.3 Operational Risk

The Committee will perform the following duties with respect to Operational Risk:

6.3.1 Provide oversight on resilience risks and trends to ensure they remain within the Bank's risk appetite

6.3.2 Provide oversight and challenge on the Bank's resilience self-assessment outputs

6.3.3 Recommend self-assessment for Board approval

6.3.4 Provide challenge on resilience vulnerabilities that have been risk accepted by the business (i.e., remediation will not be conducted)

6.3.5 Review operational risk events, near misses, and associated management actions

6.3.6 Oversee the Key Risk Indicator framework and challenge material adverse trends

### 6.4 Liquidity Risk

The Committee will perform the following duties with respect to Liquidity Risk:

6.4.1 Review liquidity risk metrics (LCR, NSFR) against limits and appetite

6.4.2 Review the results of liquidity stress testing

6.4.3 Monitor the funding profile and concentration

6.4.4 Review the adequacy of contingent funding plans

### 6.5 Model Risk

The Committee will perform the following duties with respect to Model Risk:

6.5.1 Review the model inventory and tiering

6.5.2 Oversee the model validation programme and results

6.5.3 Monitor model risk indicators and challenge material issues

6.5.4 Ensure compliance with SS1/23 model risk management requirements

### 6.6 Sustainability/Environmental Risk

The Committee is responsible to oversee the risk management approach to sustainability risk. This approach will include scenario analysis, relevant MI, and recommending risk appetite to the Board. The relevant risk sub-committees are responsible for the primary management of environmental risk that is identified through those risk types.

### 6.7 Financial Crime

Where the Committee receives financial crime related MI from its sub-committees or otherwise identifies financial crime risks, it will report such issues to the Regulatory Compliance Committee and/or any other relevant committee(s). Financial crime includes, but is not limited to, money laundering, bribery and corruption, terrorist financing, fraud, and sanctions.

### 6.8 Stress Testing

The Committee is also responsible for overseeing and reviewing stress testing. The Committee shall escalate, as necessary, any matters relating to stress testing to the Board.

## 7. Committee Operation

### 7.1 Meeting Frequency

a) A minimum of **4 scheduled meetings** a calendar year and, unless all of the Members agree otherwise, at least once in each calendar quarter

b) Additional meetings will be convened as and when necessary

c) Papers for consideration by the Committee will be submitted, in the normal course, at least **4 business days** before the meeting to enable Members to study the documentation

### 7.2 Decision Making

Each member should bring independent judgement to bear in considering matters presented to the Committee. All members are to receive all appropriate information on a timely basis.

The Chair will, where possible, evidence the approval of a decision by concluding that there is a consensus in favour of the decision and that the decision is therefore approved. In the event of no clear consensus, as determined by the Chair, a matter is required to be decided by a vote of the Committee. Any such matter shall be decided by a majority of votes. In the event of an equality of votes, the Chair shall have a casting vote.

### 7.3 Quorum

The quorum necessary for a valid meeting is at least **3 members**, including:
- At least 2 Independent Non-Executive Directors
- At least 1 Executive or Shareholder Director

### 7.4 Papers and Minutes

Materials will be sent in advance of the Committee and will normally include:
- Agenda
- Meeting paper
- Minutes (where applicable)

This will be co-ordinated by the Chair.

---

## 8. Escalation

### 8.1 To Board

The Chair will ensure the Board is informed on a timely basis of all significant matters and issues for which the Committee is responsible and any decisions it has taken.

### 8.2 Between Committees

Should the Committee identify any risk concerns which fall outside their specific mandate, the Committee has the responsibility to escalate that concern to the Chair of the appropriate committee for full consideration. In the absence of a clear path of referral, the Committee should escalate to the Chair of the Board.

### 8.3 Sub-Committees

The Committee may establish sub-committees or forums, which shall have responsibilities as determined within their approved Mandates.
