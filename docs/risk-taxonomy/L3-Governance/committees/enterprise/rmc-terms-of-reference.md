## 1. Purpose

### 1.1 Primary Responsibility

The Committee's primary responsibility is to monitor and control market risk, operational risk (including environmental risk), credit risk (including gap risk), Interest Rate Risk in the Banking Book ("IRRBB"), liquidity risk, and model risk for Meridian Global Bank ("the Bank") and to oversee adherence to the agreed Bank risk appetite.

### 1.2 Sustainability Risk

Where applicable, the Committee will consider the impact of sustainability risk, as a cross-cutting risk, on the risk types referred to in 1.1. Sustainability risk encapsulates risks across Environmental, Social, and Governance factors, as defined in the risk taxonomy.

---

## 2. Responsibilities and Delegations

### 2.1 Advisory Role

The Committee shall advise Executive Committee ("ExCo") on matters pertaining to risk management as set out in 1.1.

### 2.2 Monitoring and Oversight

The Committee is primarily responsible for monitoring and reviewing exposures of market risk, operational risk, credit risk, IRRBB, liquidity risk, and model risk for the Bank, and adherence to the agreed Bank risk appetite.

### 2.3 Breach Management

If the Chair decrees that breaches of risk appetite cannot be resolved at the Committee, the Chair has the responsibility to notify the ExCo and the Board Risk Management Committee (BRMC), proposing corrective actions/responsibilities.

### 2.4 Corrective Actions

The Committee will be informed of progress on corrective actions and will formally inform the ExCo and BRMC when the Bank is back within the agreed risk appetite.

### 2.5 Risk Appetite Changes

The Committee will review how changes in business strategy affect the agreed risk appetite for the Bank and propose to the Board a modified risk appetite in event of material change of strategy or business model.

### 2.6 ALCO Relationship

For the avoidance of doubt, ALCO is the first line of defence committee overviewing management processes to optimise the bank's deployment of balance sheet in relation to IRRBB and Liquidity Risk.

### 2.7 Information Gathering

The Committee is to receive information on exposures to market risk, operational risk, credit risk, country risk, IRRBB, liquidity risk, and model risk for the Bank and also on new business proposals or operational developments and projects from any officer, department, or other committee within the Bank, insofar as the Chair considers appropriate. It is the duty of the Chair to organise identification and collation of such information as the Committee requires in order to fulfil its responsibilities.

### 2.8 Environmental Risk

The Committee is responsible to oversee the risk management approach to sustainability risk. This approach will include scenario analysis, relevant MI, and recommending risk appetite to ExCo. The relevant risk sub-committees are responsible for the primary management of environmental risk that is identified through those risk types.

### 2.9 Operational Resilience

The Committee responsibilities in relation to operational resilience of the Bank are as follows:

2.9.1 Provide oversight on resilience risks and trends to ensure they remain within the Bank risk appetite

2.9.2 Provide oversight and challenge on the Bank's resilience self-assessment outputs

2.9.3 Recommend self-assessment for BRMC to approve

2.9.4 Provide challenge on resilience vulnerabilities that have been risk accepted by the business (i.e., remediation will not be conducted)

### 2.10 Financial Crime

Where the Committee receives financial crime related MI from its sub-committees or otherwise identifies financial crime risks, it will report such issues to the Regulatory Compliance Committee and/or any other relevant committee(s). Financial crime includes, but is not limited to, money laundering, bribery and corruption, terrorist financing, fraud, and sanctions.

### 2.11 Stress Testing

The Committee is also responsible for overseeing and reviewing stress testing. The Committee shall escalate, as necessary, any matters relating to stress testing to the ExCo and the BRMC.

### 2.12 Timely Escalation

The Chair will ensure the ExCo and the BRMC are informed on a timely basis of all significant matters and issues for which the Committee is responsible and any decisions it has taken.

### 2.13 Sub-Committee Matters

The Committee shall on a timely basis consider and decide on all matters raised for its attention by any of its sub-committees and/or forums.

### 2.14 Business Head Updates

Business Heads shall be required, periodically, to update on their risk profile or strategy as directed by the Chair.

### 2.15 Mandated Authorities

The Committee is mandated, subject to being in conformity with delegated authorities received, to:

2.15.1 Review and approve risk control policies for the Bank, as appropriate, on at least an annual basis

2.15.2 Propose changes to risk appetite for the Bank from a Credit (including Gap Risk), Market, Operational Risk, IRRBB, and Liquidity Risk perspective to the Board in the event of material change of strategy or business model

2.15.3 Demand/approve new risk treatments, metrics, and controls

### 2.16 Client and Market Impact

The Committee has a responsibility when making a decision or agreeing a course of action to be mindful of the potential impact on client outcomes, the market and the Bank (including the Bank's reputation). The Committee's responsibility extends to considering the impact over the entire life cycle of the activity/action.

### 2.17 Escalation of Concerns

Should the Committee identify any risk concerns which fall outside their specific mandate, the Committee has the responsibility to escalate that concern to the Chair of the appropriate committee for full consideration. In the absence of a clear path of referral, the Committee should escalate to the Chair of ExCo.

### 2.18 Sub-Committees

The Committee may establish sub-committees or forums, which shall have responsibilities as determined within their approved Mandates.

## 5. Committee Operation

### 5.1 Meeting Frequency

Meetings are held on a **monthly** basis, or as and when the Chair considers appropriate or necessary.

A **Contingency RMC** can be called at the discretion of the Chair, or for instance when certain metrics or indicators have been breached and requires actions to be directed by the committee (further information can be found in the appendix).

### 5.2 Independent Judgement

Each member should bring independent judgement to bear in considering matters presented to the Committee.

### 5.3 Information

All members are to receive all appropriate information on a timely basis.

### 5.4 Decision Making

The Chair will, where possible, evidence the approval of a decision by concluding that there is a consensus in favour of the decision and that the decision is therefore approved. In the event of no clear consensus, as determined by the Chair, a matter is required to be decided by a vote of the Committee. Any such matter shall be decided by a majority of votes. In the event of an equality of votes, the Chair shall have a casting vote.

### 5.5 Quorum

The quorum necessary for a valid meeting is at least **5 members**, including:
- The Chair (or Deputy Chair)
- The Head of Global Markets (or designated alternate)
- At least 3 other members

### 5.6 Papers

Materials will be sent in advance of the Committee and will normally include:
- Agenda
- Meeting papers
- Minutes (where applicable)

This will be co-ordinated by the Chair.

## 10. Delegated Limits

### 10.1 Limit Changes

| Limit Type | RMC Authority | Escalate to BRMC |
|------------|---------------|------------------|
| Entity VaR limit | Recommend only | All changes |
| Business Unit limits | ±25% of BRMC-approved | >25% change |
| Desk limits | Within BU envelope | Exceeds BU envelope |
| Single name credit limits | Up to $30m | >$30m |
| Country limits | Up to $65m | >$65m |

### 10.2 Risk Acceptances

| Risk Acceptance | RMC Authority | Escalate to BRMC |
|-----------------|---------------|------------------|
| Operational risk acceptance | Up to $6m residual | >$6m residual |
| Control deficiency acceptance | Medium-rated | High/Critical rated |
| Model limitation acceptance | Tier 2/3 models | Tier 1 models |

## 11. Contingency RMC

### 11.1 Activation Triggers

A Contingency RMC may be convened when:
- Entity VaR limit breach
- Multiple desk limit breaches
- Significant operational incident
- Model failure with capital impact
- Regulatory notification required
- Market stress event

### 11.2 Quorum for Contingency

Minimum 3 members including Chair (or Deputy) and at least one Business Head.

### 11.3 Authority

Enhanced authority during contingency to:
- Approve emergency limit changes
- Mandate immediate risk reduction
- Approve communication to regulators
- Direct business actions
