## 1. Purpose

### 1.1 Role of the Board

The Board's purpose is to ensure that Meridian Global Bank ("the Bank") is a sustainable organisation capable of fulfilling its stated objectives and maintaining its good standing with regulators. In order to fulfil its purpose, the Board must direct and control the business of the Bank.

Directors owe a fiduciary duty to the Bank and are accountable to the shareholders. Directors are also responsible, within the structure of corporate law and legislation, to the other stakeholders in the Bank. Directors are required to exercise due care and skill in the performance of their duties. All directors should exercise their powers to the benefit of the Bank.

### 1.2 Management of the Business

The business of the Bank shall be managed by the directors who may exercise all the powers of the Bank. A meeting of directors at which a quorum is present may exercise all powers exercisable by the directors.

### 1.3 Accountability

The Board is accountable to:
- Shareholders for the Bank's performance, strategy, and long-term value creation
- Regulators (PRA and FCA) for prudent management and conduct
- Other stakeholders including employees, customers, and the broader community

---

## 2. Composition

### 2.1 Size and Balance

The number of directors shall not be less than 5 nor more than 15.

| Category | Minimum | Target |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **Independent Non-Executive Directors** | 3 | At least 50% of Board |
| **Executive Directors** | 2 (CEO, CFO) | As required |
| **Non-Executive Directors** | - | As shareholder rights require |

### 2.2 Chair

The Chairman shall be an Independent Non-Executive Director. The Chairman is responsible to the Board for ensuring that the Board is able to fulfil its mandated functions.

The Chairman shall:
- Preside at every meeting of directors at which they are present
- Ensure the Board operates effectively
- Set the Board agenda with the CEO and Company Secretary
- Ensure all directors receive accurate, timely, and clear information
- Ensure effective communication with shareholders

### 2.3 Senior Independent Director

The Board shall appoint a Senior Independent Director who shall:
- Act as a sounding board for the Chairman
- Serve as an intermediary for other directors
- Be available to shareholders if they have concerns
- Lead the annual evaluation of the Chairman's performance

### 2.4 Skills and Experience

The Board will annually review a Board skills matrix considering:
- Financial services experience
- Risk management expertise
- Regulatory experience
- Technology and digital expertise
- International business experience
- ESG and sustainability expertise
- Capital markets experience

## 3. Appointment

### 3.1 Appointment Process

The Board will:

3.1.1 Make the following appointments in accordance with the Shareholders' Agreement:
- Chair of all Board committees
- Members of all Board committees
- Directors and trustees of group entities

3.1.2 Consider the removal, resignation, and retirement of directors

3.1.3 Appoint or remove the Chief Executive, other Executive Directors, and the Company Secretary

### 3.2 Rotation and Term

3.2.1 Ensure the proper rotation of directors in terms of the Articles of Association

3.2.2 All directors shall stand for re-election annually at the Annual General Meeting

3.2.3 Non-Executive Directors shall normally serve for a maximum of 9 years

### 3.3 Skills Assessment

3.3.1 Ensure that the Chair and members of committees have the adequate skills and knowledge, both individually and collectively, to perform their functions and fulfil their responsibilities

3.3.2 Annually review a Board skills matrix and consider the linkage with training, succession planning, and future appointments

### 3.4 Independence Assessment

3.4.1 The Board will assess the independence of Non-Executive Directors annually

3.4.2 Independence considerations include:
- Previous employment with the Bank
- Material business relationships
- Family relationships with executives
- Cross-directorships
- Length of service

---

## 4. Quorum

The quorum for the transaction of the business of the directors shall be:
- At least **5 directors**
- Including at least **2 Independent Non-Executive Directors**
- Including at least **1 Executive Director** when the relevant business is transacted

---

## 5. Director Responsibilities

### 5.1 Fiduciary Duties

Directors must:
- Act in good faith to promote the success of the Bank
- Exercise independent judgement
- Exercise reasonable care, skill, and diligence
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Not accept benefits from third parties
- Declare interest in proposed transactions

### 5.2 Statutory Duties (Section 172 Considerations)

In promoting the success of the Bank, directors must have regard to:
- The likely consequences of any decision in the long term
- The interests of the Bank's employees
- The need to foster business relationships with suppliers, customers, and others
- The impact of operations on the community and the environment
- The desirability of maintaining a reputation for high standards of business conduct
- The need to act fairly as between shareholders

### 5.3 Non-Executive Director Duties

Non-Executive Directors should:
- Hold management to account against the matters delegated
- Be able to challenge the executive effectively and promptly
- Bring independent perspective to Board deliberations
- Provide constructive challenge to strategy and performance

### 5.4 Regulatory Requirements

3.6 The Prudential Regulation Authority, the Financial Conduct Authority, or other relevant regulatory body has the right to object to the appointment of a director, or to terminate the service of an existing director, if they believe that they are not fit and proper or not acting in the public interest.

## 6. Authority, Powers of Delegation, and Terms of Reference

The Board shall:

### 6.1 Strategy and Objectives

6.1.1 Agree the Bank's objectives and purpose

6.1.2 Agree the strategies and plans for achieving those objectives, ensuring consistency with overall Group strategy

6.1.3 Monitor and periodically assess the effectiveness of the governance arrangements and take steps to address any deficiencies

6.1.4 Review its mandate every 2 years and approve recommended changes

### 6.2 Delegation

6.2.1 Delegate to the Chief Executive or any director any of the powers, authorities, and directions vested in the Board's directors, including the power of sub-delegation

6.2.2 Delegate similarly such powers, authorities, and discretions to any committee and subsidiary company boards as may exist or be created from time to time

6.2.3 Determine the terms of reference and procedures of all Board committees and subsidiary companies and review their minutes and reports where appropriate

### 6.3 Oversight

6.3.1 Consider and evaluate reports submitted by management

6.3.2 Ensure that an effective risk management process exists and is maintained

6.3.3 Ensure a robust and prudent risk appetite statement is formulated, signed off, and monitored, and ensure that breaches are identified and corrective actions implemented

6.3.4 Approve the Operational Resilience self-assessment document and provide oversight of any necessary regulatory disclosures

6.3.5 Review and evaluate any parent-level decisions or practices to ensure that they do not put the regulated subsidiary in breach of applicable local legal or regulatory provisions

6.3.6 Review and monitor the performance of the Chief Executive and the executive team in light of the Bank's strategy, objectives, business plans, and budget

### 6.4 Governance and People

6.4.1 Ensure consideration is given to succession planning for both the Chief Executive and other Executive Directors and executive management

6.4.2 Establish, review from time to time, and approve major changes to the Bank's key policies or make recommendations to the Shareholders where appropriate

6.4.3 Note the remuneration to be paid to Non-Executive Directors on the Board and Board committees based on recommendations made by the Remuneration Committee

6.4.4 Ensure that Directors have engaged with employees and taken account of their interests

6.4.5 Ensure that Directors have engaged with suppliers, customers, and others in a business relationship with the Bank

### 6.5 Financial

6.5.1 Approve capital funding for the Bank and Group and the terms and conditions of rights or other issues

6.5.2 Approve capital expenditure budgets for the Bank and its subsidiaries

6.5.3 Consider and recommend to Shareholders for approval any significant changes proposed in accounting policy or practice and consider the recommendations of the Audit Committee

6.5.4 Consider and approve the external audit fee and budgeted audit fee as per the recommendation of the Audit Committee

6.5.5 Consider and approve the annual financial statements, interim statements, and dividend proposals

6.5.6 Have ultimate responsibility for the integrity of accounting and financial reporting systems, including operational and internal controls, the adequacy and review of which will be delegated to sub-committees

### 6.6 Legal and Regulatory Compliance

6.6.1 Have ultimate responsibility for regulatory and legal compliance (including but not limited to, compliance with applicable laws and regulations on anti-money laundering, anti-bribery and corruption, counter terrorist financing, fraud, sanctions, and any other financial crimes)

6.6.2 Ensure that reporting to the Board and/or Board committees is comprehensive across prudential requirements as well as conduct of business

### 6.7 Culture and ESG

6.7.1 Under the leadership of the Chair, the Board is responsible for articulating and maintaining a culture of risk awareness and ethical behaviour for the Bank to follow in pursuit of its business goals

6.7.2 Oversee the Bank's responsibilities and response to Environmental (including climate change), Social, and Governance ("ESG") matters, including:
- Setting strategic direction and risk appetite for sustainability-related risks
- Monitoring the implementation of such strategy
- Setting and monitoring sustainability targets and metrics
- Providing oversight and approval of sustainability reporting and disclosures

6.7.3 Oversee the process of disclosure and communications

### 6.8 Corporate Transactions

6.8.1 To consider and recommend to Shareholders' approval (where required) of significant acquisitions, mergers, take-overs, divestments of operating companies, equity investments, and new strategic alliances. To approve the above matters where shareholder approval is not required.

### 6.9 Director Authority

6.9.1 Directors shall have authority to:
- Access any information they need to fulfil their responsibilities
- Seek independent advice at the Bank's expense
- Investigate matters within the Board mandate
- Consult with, and receive the full co-operation of, any employee where necessary to fulfil responsibilities

## 7. Board Operation

### 7.1 Meetings

7.1.1 A minimum of **6 scheduled meetings** a calendar year and, unless all of the directors agree otherwise, at least once in each calendar quarter

7.1.2 Additional meetings will be convened as and when necessary

7.1.3 Resolutions of the directors shall be decided by a majority of votes. In the case of an equality of votes, the Chair shall have a second or casting vote.

### 7.2 Agenda and Papers

7.2.1 The Chair, with the assistance of the Company Secretary, shall establish the agenda for each Board meeting and ensure that any additional agenda items from the Chief Executive and other directors are incorporated

7.2.2 Papers for consideration by the Board will be submitted, in the normal course, at least **4 business days** before the meeting to enable directors to study the documentation

### 7.3 Minutes

7.3.1 Minutes of all Board meetings shall record the proceedings and decisions taken

7.3.2 The Company Secretary shall take the minutes. In the absence of the Company Secretary, their delegate shall take the minutes subject to the agreement of the Chair.

### 7.4 Conflicts of Interest

7.4.1 Full disclosure of the nature of a director's interest on any matter before the Board is required

7.4.2 A director may vote only in accordance with the Bank's Articles of Association on any matter in which they have an interest. Additionally, a director may be recused from any meeting when matters in which they have an interest are discussed.

### 7.5 External Expertise

7.5.1 The Board may request the attendance of management or outside expertise at Board meetings at specific or regular times to provide the Board with required information and expert insight into matters being deliberated

The Board receives reports from:

|-----------|-----------|---------|

| Audit Committee | Quarterly | Audit findings, control issues, financial reporting |

### 8.2 To Shareholders

| Report | Frequency | Content |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Annual Report | Annually | Strategy, performance, governance |
| Interim Results | Half-yearly | Financial performance |
| AGM | Annually | Shareholder engagement |

## 9. Matters Reserved for the Board

The following matters are reserved for the Board's decision:

### 9.1 Strategy and Management
- Overall strategic direction and objectives
- Annual budget and business plan
- Major changes to corporate structure
- Material contracts outside ordinary course

### 9.2 Financial
- Annual and interim financial statements
- Dividend policy and declarations
- Major capital expenditure (>$10m)
- Treasury policy

### 9.3 Risk and Compliance
- Risk appetite statement
- ICAAP and ILAAP approval
- Recovery and Resolution Plan
- Regulatory notifications and correspondence

### 9.4 Governance
- Board and committee composition
- CEO and Executive Director appointments
- Company Secretary appointment
- Delegation of authority framework

### 9.5 Corporate Actions
- Acquisitions, mergers, disposals (>$25m)
- Major litigation settlement
- Significant regulatory settlements

---

## 10. Effectiveness Review

### 10.1 Annual Evaluation

The Board will conduct an annual evaluation covering:
- Board composition and balance
- Quality of information and papers
- Decision-making effectiveness
- Board dynamics and culture
- Committee effectiveness

### 10.2 External Evaluation

An externally facilitated Board evaluation shall be conducted at least every 3 years.

### 10.3 Individual Director Evaluation

The Chairman shall evaluate individual director performance annually, with feedback provided to each director.
