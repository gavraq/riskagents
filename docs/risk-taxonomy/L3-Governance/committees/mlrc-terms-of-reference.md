## 1. Purpose and Authority

### 1.1 Purpose

The Market & Liquidity Risk Committee (MLRC) is established to provide oversight of the Bank's market risk and liquidity risk exposures. It serves as the primary forum for monitoring trading book risks, reviewing limit utilisation, and ensuring compliance with the Market Risk Policy and Liquidity Risk Policy.

### 1.2 Authority

The MLRC derives its authority from the Risk Management Committee (RMC) and operates under delegated authority to:

- Monitor market risk and liquidity risk against approved limits
- Approve limit changes within delegated thresholds
- Approve temporary limit excesses within delegated parameters
- Recommend policy changes to RMC
- Escalate material issues to RMC and/or CRO

### 1.3 Delegation Limits

| Action | MLRC Authority | Escalate to RMC |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Desk VaR limit change | ±20% of current | >20% change |
| Temporary excess approval | Up to 10% for 5 days | >10% or >5 days |
| New product approval | Standard products | Complex/material products |
| Methodology change | Minor calibration | Fundamental change |

Standing substitutes may be nominated in writing. Substitutes have full voting rights when attending in place of a member.

## 3. Meeting Arrangements

### 3.1 Frequency

| Meeting Type | Frequency | Duration |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| Regular MLRC | Weekly (every Monday) | 90 minutes |
| Extraordinary MLRC | As required | As needed |

### 3.2 Agenda

Standard agenda items:

1. **Approval of Previous Minutes** (5 min)
2. **Action Item Review** (10 min)
3. **Market Risk Update** (20 min)
   - VaR summary and trends
   - Limit utilisation
   - Backtesting results
   - Exceptions and breaches
4. **Liquidity Risk Update** (15 min)
   - LCR/NSFR status
   - Funding position
   - Liquidity stress results
5. **Stress Testing Results** (15 min)
   - Weekly scenario results
   - Emerging risk scenarios
6. **Approvals Required** (15 min)
   - Limit changes
   - Temporary excesses
   - New products
7. **AOB and Escalations** (10 min)

### 3.3 Papers

- Papers to be circulated **2 business days** before meeting
- Late papers only with Chair approval
- Standard pack includes:
  - VaR Dashboard
  - Limit Utilisation Report
  - Backtesting Report
  - Stress Testing Summary
  - Exception Report
  - Approval Requests

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Market Risk Oversight

| Responsibility | Frequency | Output |
|----------------|-----------|--------|
| Review VaR exposures vs limits | Weekly | Dashboard review |
| Monitor backtesting performance | Weekly | Exception tracking |
| Review stress test results | Weekly | Stress report |
| Approve desk limit changes | As needed | Limit change memo |
| Review model performance | Monthly | Model MI |
| Review concentration exposures | Weekly | Concentration report |

### 4.2 Liquidity Risk Oversight

| Responsibility | Frequency | Output |
|----------------|-----------|--------|
| Review LCR/NSFR vs limits | Weekly | Liquidity dashboard |
| Monitor funding profile | Weekly | Funding report |
| Review liquidity stress tests | Weekly | Stress results |
| Review intraday liquidity | Monthly | Intraday report |

### 4.3 Approvals

The MLRC is authorised to approve:

| Item | Approval Threshold |
|------|-------------------|
| Desk VaR limit changes | Within ±20% of RMC-approved limits |
| Temporary limit excesses | Up to 10% for maximum 5 business days |
| New product trading (standard) | Pre-defined product types |
| Minor methodology changes | Calibration updates |
| Data source changes | Non-material changes |

### 4.4 Escalation Duties

The MLRC must escalate to RMC:

- Entity VaR limit breach
- 4+ backtesting exceptions in rolling 250 days
- Material model issues
- Regulatory findings related to market/liquidity risk
- Emerging risks requiring policy change
- Requests exceeding MLRC delegation

## 6. Decision Making

### 6.1 Voting

- Decisions by simple majority of voting members present
- Chair has casting vote in case of tie
- Dissenting views to be recorded in minutes

### 6.2 Decision Record

All decisions must be recorded with:
- Decision made
- Rationale
- Any conditions attached
- Dissenting views (if any)
- Effective date

### 6.3 Decisions Between Meetings

For urgent matters between meetings:
- Chair may approve via email with majority member consent
- Decision ratified at next MLRC meeting
- Full audit trail maintained

---

## 7. Minutes and Records

### 7.1 Minutes

Minutes must include:
- Attendance
- Apologies
- Previous minutes approval
- Discussion summary (not verbatim)
- Decisions made with rationale
- Action items with owners and due dates
- Escalations raised

### 7.2 Distribution

| Recipient | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Members | Draft within 3 days |
| Final minutes | Within 5 days post-approval |
| RMC (summary) | Monthly |

### 7.3 Retention

- Minutes retained for 7 years
- Supporting papers retained for 7 years
- Stored in Document Management System
